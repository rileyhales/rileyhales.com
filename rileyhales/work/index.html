Research Assistant - BYU Hydroinformatics Lab

April 2018 - Present

Our goal is to provide water and environmental modeling tools, especially for use in less-developed areas of the world. Traditional methods of water and environmental modeling are important there but, because of the cost, expertise, infrastructure and time requirements, are often inaccessible or not feasible. We use remote sensing and GIS data to run our models and provide access to them through a graphical user interface by writing simple web applications. These tools are built on free and open source software and data so they can be applied globaly at little if any cost. See worldwater.byu.edu for more about our lab.

ASCE BYU Chapter Officers - Networking Committee

April 2018 - April 2019

I was elected to the BYU ASCE Student Chapter's Networking Committee. Seeking to help students market themselves to recruiters and be better prepared for post-graduation plans, our ASCE chapter decided to form a networking committee. Our goal was to connect students with businesses and get support in related skills such as interviewing, building a LinkedIn profile, and writing resumes. The highlight of our service were two networking dinner events where we hosted recruiters from 17 companies and almost 100 students for a 3 hour dinner and social event. These and our other events were the first largely successful networking events attempted by our student chapter in many years.
